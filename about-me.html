About Art

The only institutional training I received was from one semester of art class in high school. The rest of my skills were formed after watching my father show my siblings and I some sketches he did of comic book characters, which turned into all of us, my dad included, drawing on sheets of lined notebook paper. I saw the lines he drew turn into realistic-looking fingers, arms, legs, a head, and eventually a sketch of a man posed dynamically, as seen on superheroes. His drawings were so beautiful and life-like, it was the prettiest thing 5-year-old me had ever seen. From there on, I wanted to draw, too. It's a nice escape when life gets too overwhelming and stressful. Art is my life and I'm incredibly grateful for ability and willpower to draw. I wasn't born knowing how to draw, but I got there with a lifetime of practice done during my free time, continuously perfecting the skill I'm looking for, which is, to this day, comic book style. A combination of the art styles found in Naruto and El Eternauta.
